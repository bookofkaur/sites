Why STEM Is the Future — Even If You Don't Think You're a "Math Person"

By Darrian Belcher5 min readCareer · Education

STEM is the future. Without it, society wouldn't progress. That sounds like a bold claim — but think about it: every single day, innovations in science, technology, engineering, and mathematics shape every aspect of your life. The food you eat. The car you ride in. The music you stream. The phone in your pocket. Science is everywhere.

You Don't Have to Be a "Math Person" to Belong in STEM

One of the biggest lies we tell students is that STEM is only for a certain type of person — the kid who aced calculus, who loves equations, who never struggles. That's not how it works.

I struggled. I sat through assignments that made me feel defeated. I had moments where I genuinely didn't think I could do it. But a teacher told me to treat every challenge as an opportunity for improvement — and that changed everything.

The real requirement for STEM isn't being a "math person." It's being a curious person. A persistent person. Someone who wants to understand how things work and make them work better.

How Math and Science Show Up in Tech Careers

If you're considering a career in technology, here's how these subjects actually come up on the job:

  • Algorithms — the logic that powers every app you use — are built on discrete mathematics and algebra.
  • Debugging code is structurally identical to reviewing a calculus problem: you work backwards to find where the logic broke.
  • Data analysis uses statistics to tell stories that drive business decisions.
  • Physics and engineering are the backbone of hardware, robotics, and systems design.

In a computational physics course I took in high school, I used Python to simulate kinetic collisions and calculate velocity. I didn't think of it as "doing physics" — I thought of it as solving an interesting puzzle with code.

STEM Is Where the World's Hardest Problems Get Solved

Cancer research. Alzheimer's treatment. Renewable energy. Artificial intelligence. Autonomous vehicles. These aren't just tech buzzwords — they're the defining challenges of our generation, and STEM is the toolkit that addresses them.

"Some of the most impactful causes being researched today all require a combination of STEM fields. STEM is innovation, and its importance to societal progress can't be overstated."

STEM Careers Are Growing Faster Than Almost Everything Else

The Bureau of Labor Statistics consistently ranks STEM occupations among the fastest-growing and highest-paying fields. But beyond salary, STEM gives you leverage — the ability to build things, automate things, analyze things, and shape things that affect millions of people.

You don't have to start there. You just have to start curious.

💡 For Students Who Are on the Fence: You don't have to major in computer science to use technology meaningfully. Healthcare + data. Art + code. Education + AI. Some of the most impactful careers sit at the intersection of STEM and the thing you already love.
